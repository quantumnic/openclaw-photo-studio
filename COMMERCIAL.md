# Commercial Licensing — OpenClaw Photo Studio

## When Do I Need a Commercial License?

You need a commercial license if you:

- **Embed** OCPS (or parts of it) into commercial software
- **Offer** OCPS as a SaaS or managed service
- **Sell** a product that includes OCPS as a core component
- **White-label** OCPS under a different brand
- **Deploy** OCPS to more than 50 workstations in an enterprise

## When Do I NOT Need a Commercial License?

- ✅ Using OCPS to edit your own photos (even professionally)
- ✅ Personal use, learning, research
- ✅ Non-profit organizations
- ✅ Companies with ≤50 users for internal photo editing
- ✅ Developing and selling independent plugins
- ✅ Developing and selling presets
- ✅ Non-commercial forks and modifications
- ✅ Education (free up to 100 seats)

## Common Questions

**I'm a wedding photographer. Do I need a license?**
No. You're an end user. Use OCPS freely.

**I sell Lightroom presets. Can I convert them for OCPS?**
Yes. Presets are your own creative work. No license needed.

**I want to build an online photo editor using the OCPS engine.**
That's SaaS use. You need a commercial license. Contact us.

**I'm building a camera app and want to use the RAW engine.**
That's OEM embedding. You need a commercial license.

**Can I offer paid OCPS support and training?**
Generally yes, but contact us for confirmation.

**I run a photo school with 200 students.**
Education license: free up to 100 seats, €10/seat/year above that.
